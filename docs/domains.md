# Domains

<!-- source: https://www.tacitus.me/research/tcgc -->

7 conflict domains. The scope statements below are verbatim from the TCGC specification.

---

## `workplace`

HR grievances, performance disputes, promotion blocks.

**Typical documents:** Slack/email threads, performance reviews, HR correspondence.

**Typical actors:** Individual employees, managers, HR representatives, legal counsel.

**Conflict archetypes:** Commitment-vs-denial of verbal agreement; performance assessment dispute; promotion block with unstated constraint.

---

## `commercial`

Contract breach, vendor dispute, joint-venture friction.

**Typical documents:** Negotiation transcripts, contracts, email chains, term sheets.

**Typical actors:** Vendor, buyer, legal teams, procurement.

**Conflict archetypes:** Position/interest divergence in pricing negotiation; disputed delivery milestone; joint-venture governance deadlock.

---

## `governance`

Board disagreement, mandate conflict, committee deadlock.

**Typical documents:** Board minutes, committee reports, governance policies.

**Typical actors:** Board directors, chairs, executives, committee members.

**Conflict archetypes:** Contradictory minutes on a key decision; mandate dispute between committees; procedural challenge to voting outcome.

---

## `peace-process`

Ceasefire, DDR, political-track multilateral negotiation.

**Typical documents:** Diplomatic memos, track-II summaries, communiqués.

**Typical actors:** State delegations, mediators, civil society observers, international bodies.

**Conflict archetypes:** Disputed event sequence in a ceasefire timeline; commitment revocation in DDR process; framing shift between Track I and Track II.

---

## `policy`

Regulatory contestation, stakeholder reception, public-consultation aftermath.

**Typical documents:** Policy briefs, consultation submissions, regulatory filings, press releases.

**Typical actors:** Regulators, industry groups, civil society, government ministries.

**Conflict archetypes:** Contradictory stakeholder claims on impact; contested timeline of regulatory action; interest/position split in lobbying communication.

---

## `family`

Inheritance, custody arrangement, intergenerational wealth dispute.

**Typical documents:** Legal correspondence, mediation transcripts, wills, family agreements.

**Typical actors:** Family members, legal representatives, mediators.

**Conflict archetypes:** Disputed oral commitment on inheritance; custody arrangement contested after circumstances change; contradictory interpretations of a prior agreement.

---

## `diplomatic`

State-to-state friction, border incident, multilateral block formation.

**Typical documents:** Press briefings, diplomatic cables, UN statements, multilateral communiqués.

**Typical actors:** State foreign ministries, diplomatic representatives, multilateral bodies.

**Conflict archetypes:** Contradictory claims between two states on a bilateral issue; disputed provenance of a statement; block formation around a contested multilateral agenda item.
